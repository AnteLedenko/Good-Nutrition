What is a Good Nutrition?

Eating a regular, balanced diet is the fundamental component of nutrition. A healthy diet provides your body with fuel. The nutrients your body needs to sustain healthy brain, muscles, bones, nerves, skin, blood circulation, and immune system are found in the foods you eat.

A healthy diet also lessens your risk of developing illnesses and diseases like osteoporosis, diabetes, cancer, and heart disease.

Macronutrients and micronutrients are the two main categories of nutrients found in food. Carbohydrates, protein, and fat are macronutrients. They provide energy (in the form of calories) and act as the tissues' and muscles' building blocks.

Macronutrients

The "macros" are the nutrients that your body requires in significant quantities in order to perform at its best. The three primary macronutrients are fat, protein, and carbs.

Although there are recommended ranges for macronutrient intake, your needs vary based on your personal circumstances.

Micronutrients

Vitamins and minerals are referred to as micronutrients; they are further classified as macrominerals, trace minerals, and water- and fat-soluble vitamins. A balanced diet is frequently the goal in order to obtain an adequate number of micronutrients.
