What are macronutrients?

In order for your body to perform at its best, it needs a lot of macronutrients.

There are three primary macronutrients: fat, protein, and carbs. Because they are regarded as necessary nutrients, your body is either unable to produce them or cannot produce enough of them.

Essential fatty acids, for instance, are found in fats, whereas essential amino acids are found in proteins. Your body uses certain substances for particular purposes.

Calories are another kind of energy found in macronutrients. Your body uses carbs as its primary energy source, but it can also use other macronutrients if necessary.

Carbs functions

Instant energy. Your central nervous system, brain, and red blood cells all prefer to utilise glucose as an energy source.

Storing energy. Your muscles and liver store glucose as glycogen, which you can utilize later on when you need energy for instance, after a prolonged fast.

Digestion. Fiber encourages regular bowel motions.

Helps you experience fullness. Following a meal, fiber makes you feel fuller for longer.

Protein functions

Building and repairing. Your body uses amino acids to help make new proteins. Additionally, they help to maintain and heal muscles and tissues.

Providing structure. The skin, hair, nails, organs, and cell membranes in your body are all made of amino acids.

Balance of pH. Your body needs amino acids to keep the right acid-base balance.

Producing hormones and enzymes. The proper amino acids are necessary for your body to produce hormones and enzymes.

Fat functions

Health of the cell membrane. Cell membranes require lipids as a fundamental component.

Storing energy. When you eat less calories than you burn, the fat that is stored throughout your body can be used as a reservoir of energy.

Transport and absorption. Lipids aid in the transportation of fat-soluble vitamins K, E, D, and A and facilitate their absorption.

Insulation. Your organs are insulated and protected by fat.
