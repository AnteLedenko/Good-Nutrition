What are Micronutrients?

Micronutrients is the general word for vitamins and minerals.

Vitamins are natural compounds that are produced by animals and plants and are broken by air, acid, and heat . Minerals, on the other hand, are inorganic substances that can only be found in water or soil.

To ensure you are getting adequate vitamins and minerals, it is best to eat a range of foods because each item has a different amount of micronutrients.

Vitamins and minerals are essential for development, immunological response, brain function, and many other critical processes.

Types of Macronutrients

Four categories exist for vitamins and minerals: water-soluble vitamins, fat-soluble vitamins, macrominerals and trace minerals.

Minerals and vitamins, no matter what kind, are absorbed in your body similarly and work together in several functions.
